> *Mens sana in corpore sano.*
> <br><small>&mdash; Juvenal, *Satires* X</small>

A healthy mind in a healthy body. This ancient observation is not mere folk wisdom&mdash;it is one of the most replicated findings in cognitive neuroscience. Aerobic exercise drives hippocampal neurogenesis, strengthens prefrontal executive function, and upregulates BDNF, the very molecular machinery of learning and memory. For someone who spends their days modeling the computational architecture of cognition, the irony is hard to miss: the brain I study works better when the body it inhabits is pushed to its limits.

I have found that the clarity that follows intense physical effort is qualitatively different from rest. It is not relaxation&mdash;it is a kind of recalibration. The sustained attention needed to formalize a proof or debug a parser is the same faculty that sharpens when cardiovascular fitness improves. Embodied cognition is not just a theory I read about; it is something I experience every time I step off the field or out of the water and return to a problem with fresh eyes.

### Football

Football is a laboratory in real-time decision-making. Every touch is a rapid inference under uncertainty&mdash;reading the spatial configuration of teammates and opponents, predicting trajectories, selecting actions under tight temporal constraints. It is multi-agent coordination at its most intuitive, the kind of fluid problem-solving that formal models of cognition aspire to capture.

### Surfing

Surfing is pattern recognition in a stochastic environment. You learn to read the ocean&mdash;the period of the swell, the shape of the sandbar, the subtle cues that distinguish a wave worth committing to from one that will close out. There is a meditative quality to waiting in the lineup, a state of relaxed vigilance that resembles the attentional stance of a Bayesian observer: maintaining priors, updating on evidence, and committing decisively when the posterior shifts.
